22 for 2022

"Here is a list of 22 pieces I think oboists should be playing and teaching in 2022. They should have and may have been enjoyed (sometimes long) before this. As I am terrible at list making and it causes me anxiety, I actually deliver 25 composers here instead of 22. Think of my counting as approximate. And for some composers, I list multiple pieces. Brevity is not my strong suit. If I don't specify instrumentation, it's for oboe/piano or oboe solo.

Yesterday, I made another imperfect list of ***THE 31 Most Popular Works for Oboe Lessons, Recitals, Competitions, and School Auditions***. I was thinking of including a token piece by somebody who was a woman and/or a person of color because we definitely see people trying to branch out on occasion. But I just couldn't think of any options that were as truly mainstream as the rest of the pieces on that list. There are a number of pieces on that first list I truly love, and there are a few I cannot abide. If you've played all or most of the pieces on my ***31 Most Popular*** list, or even a decent fraction of them but only ever a token piece or few by a woman composer and/or a composer of color, please check out my ***22 for 2022*** list. I see so many students playing one or two movements of many of the pieces from my **31 Most Popular List*** because the whole pieces are just too hard for them (Poulenc, Saint-Säens, Vaughan-Williams, Schumann, Marcello, Hindemith, Mozart, Strauss.) Instead of studying something you won't be able to play in its entirety yet, I've put asterisks on pieces in this newest list which might be currently manageable if you can handle the easiest movements of some of the big pieces in the ***31 Most Popular.***

I have very good to loving feelings about every piece on this list. This is more than I can say for my ***31 Most Popular List***. I am sure this list is wildly imperfect and missing a ton; it was hard for me to keep it as short as it is. It's always exciting to find new, great pieces that have been either completely ignored or not given their due. Please let me know what you think I should've put here. Maybe it will find its way onto another list!"


- Katherine Needleman

Reedmaking resources

PDFs

  • Linda Strommen and Elaine Douvas reed class PDFs

    • Reed Class I - introduces goals while reedmaking, reviews some objective tests to conduct when evaluating the reed, reasons why loose sides destroy your reeds and how to avoid it, step by step instruction on a reed from cane to clipping, and a helpful chart at the end on hard/weak/sharp/flat reeds.

    • Reed Class II - more discussion of reed tests, helpful chart on the last page.

  • Reed Solutions Chart - descriptions and pictures of where to scrape for a variety of reed troubles or aspirations.

  • Carolyn Hove's 4 day English Horn routine - a comprehensive guide to making your English Horn reeds, with pictures and measurements

Videos

  • Pierre Roy

    Excellent demonstrator, good camera angles, and plenty of videos to pick from, each often targetting a specific thing to focus on while making a reed. Some selections:

    • Pitch before Tone and Slope before Sides - an order of operations to consider when beginning a reed

    • Peep Rattle and Crow - what sounds you should be looking for after your beginning scrapes

    • Finishing the Tip - some instructions when it comes to the final steps of getting your reed where you need it at the tip

    Check out his website for things like his shaper tips, too.

  • Making an Oboe Reed by Brooks Fisher

    A lengthy, but in-depth start to finish video on how to make a reed, with lots of helpful knowledge and guidelines to adhere to throughout.

  • Geoffrey Johnson on Double Hollow Ground Knife Sharpening

    I had difficulty finding a video that had a demonstration of the exact steps for knife sharpening in the way that I was first taught - using a stone, crock sticks, and finishing with a burnishing rod. Geoffrey Johnson's video perfectly showcases the first two in a brief, but descriptive video.

Places to buy from

Note - any websites or stores not listed are not to be avoided, but were most likely omitted due to not filling a good enough niche or not being the most affordable for what they offer

  • oboe-shop.de

    Despite being in Germany, I found the shipping price to be reasonable. Of course, the shipping time may be a few weeks, but if you order in advance the prices make it worth it.

      Tube cane - I have found their tube cane cost to be really rather affordable compared to almost every US store. Lots of budget cane options too, like the Rouché and Carmen options. Vic is notably cheaper on here than most US stores.

  • Squirrely Stash

    Unmatched in unique, rotating, beautiful thread.

  • MusEcho

    This company is producing innovative oboe tools that have the potential to become standard in the future.

    • Ruler / radius gauge - this product is a ruler with important measurement markings, while also having radius gauge diameters on it.

    • Reed thread - this thread has great color options, a reliable spool, and comes prewaxed.

  • Roger Miller

    • Burnishing Rod - Roger Miller's burnishing rod is a great tool for keeping your knives sharp. Comfortable handle.

  • Innoledy

    Great prices on a lot of items. Their cane used to be lower than many other sellers, but it appears to have gone up. It might still be lower than other stores, but will need additional checking. The Innoledy Chinese cane is affordable for beginners. They offer free shipping at as low as $50, which is a great offer. Can have long shipping times and can be difficult to get in contact with. Products to check out:

    • Wilson tool cases - good price on these great travel tool bags.

    • Guercio mandrel - low price to go with their Guercio Glotin copies
    • Ceramic (crock) sticks - a good price for small or regular length crock sticks

    • Seems like they have low prices on their easel and cutting blocks.

    • While the thread has great color selections and a very long length for the price, I would suggest avoiding their thread, as the spool is only stopped on one side. More than once I had the thread get tangled up because of the lack of a double-sided stopper and ended up needing to cut off a huge chunk of the thread.

  • Advantage Double Reeds

    They sell some cane, staples, and other tools, but some notable products include:

    • Hiniker staples - the only store that sells them, I believe

    • Vitry knife - if you are looking for a rubber handle, this one would do great, and is at a good price

  • Hodge Products

    They sell lots of products, and often have sales, which a lot of other websites rarely provide. A great product from them is this 20-reed case, which you will not find at this low of a price likely anywhere else. It holds up strong despite its price, and you can even pick from 3 different finishes.

  • Mark Chudnow

    The Chudnow English Horn staples are my favorite to use on English Horn as they have a nicer shape and are easier to hold and handle on the instrument.

  • Nielsen Woodwinds

    The Nielsen Knife is strikingly affordable and has several different handle sizes. It scrapes well and I'm able to use it for tip work and other full scrapes. By far my favorite knife at less than half the cost of something like a Landwell.
