Oboe

Elijah Barrios is an oboist who is currently studying with Dr. Eric Ohlsson at Florida State University, pursuing his Bachelor's in Music Performance while also dual majoring in Computer Science. Since coming to FSU, Elijah has been a member of the FSU Wind Orchestra, the top graduate wind band.

Throughout high school, Elijah was a consistent Florida All State participant. During his junior year, he participated in the MusicForAll Honor Band of America. Outside of school and band programs, he played English Horn and Principal Oboe with the Florida Symphony Youth Orchestra.

Prior to coming to Tallahassee, Elijah previously studied with Ryan Roberts, Alexandra Knoll, and Erik Cole.

Reedmaking resources

PDFs

  • Linda Strommen and Elaine Douvas reed class PDFs

    • Reed Class I - introduces goals while reedmaking, reviews some objective tests to conduct when evaluating the reed, reasons why loose sides destroy your reeds and how to avoid it, step by step instruction on a reed from cane to clipping, and a helpful chart at the end on hard/weak/sharp/flat reeds.

    • Reed Class II - more discussion of reed tests, helpful chart on the last page.

  • Reed Solutions Chart - descriptions and pictures of where to scrape for a variety of reed troubles or aspirations.

Videos

  • Pierre Roy

    Excellent demonstrator, good camera angles, and plenty of videos to pick from, each often targetting a specific thing to focus on while making a reed. Some selections:

    • Pitch before Tone and Slope before Sides - an order of operations to consider when beginning a reed

    • Peep Rattle and Crow - what sounds you should be looking for after your beginning scrapes

    • Finishing the Tip - some instructions when it comes to the final steps of getting your reed where you need it at the tip

    Check out his website for things like his shaper tips, too.

  • Making an Oboe Reed by Brooks Fisher

    A lengthy, but in-depth start to finish video on how to make a reed, with lots of helpful knowledge and guidelines to adhere to throughout.

  • Geoffrey Johnson on Double Hollow Ground Knife Sharpening

    I had difficulty finding a video that had a demonstration of the exact steps for knife sharpening in the way that I was first taught - using a stone, crock sticks, and finishing with a burnishing rod. Geoffrey Johnson's video perfectly showcases the first two in a brief, but descriptive video.
